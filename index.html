About

I'm a Data Science and Economics student at the University of Virginia — not a traditional finance student, not a pure CS student. The combination is intentional: I'm drawn to problems where models and decisions have real stakes.

I'm most interested in machine learning and applied AI — how models actually get built, deployed, and used to make decisions. That interest spans sports analytics, quantitative finance, and increasingly the question of what it means to build AI responsibly.

The projects here are self-directed, deployed, and tested — not class assignments. I care about the full stack: from raw data to a running system that produces real output.

I'm also pursuing undergraduate research in data ethics and AI policy through UVA's School of Data Science. It's the kind of question that shows up everywhere once you start looking — and I think it matters more than most people in the field want to admit.
